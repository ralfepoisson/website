About me

I grew up in Durban, South Africa, and started playing around with Turbo Pascal 7 and VB6 when I was 12. From there, I started working as a junior programmer for a large textile manufacturer in my school holidays. After school I studied Comp Sci and IT, and was a guest lecturer at a few Durban-based universities, giving lectures to undergraduates and MBA students on a variety of topics from Open Source Web Development to Strategic Corporate Security. I have bee involved in a number of other software-related start-ups, and run the local chapter of ISG.

Apart from IT, I also have qualifications in Psychology, and currently am conducting research on the Deaf Community in South Africa. I was involved in a pilot project for psychosocial interventions with Deaf children sponsored by Sinomlando. I am fluent in South African Sign Language and engage in volunteer work as a Sign Language interpreter.

I also play a number of instruments orchestraly: Piano, Violin, Cello. I am currently a member of the Durban City Orchestra, playing first violin.
